Optical Camera-based Touch Interface

A low-cost touch interface solution

converting any surface into a touchable interface with relatively low cost.

Touch interface have became one of the most popular topics in the field of HCI. Scholars have adopted various ways to achieve responsible multitouch interfaces and introduced it to various aspects of daily use. Nowadays, the technology for small scale touch interface, for example, capacitive touch screen, is very mature. However, the technologies for small-scale touch interface could not be adopted to larger interactive platform due to the significant increase in cost and production difficulty.

The combination of large screen and touch interaction opens potential markets such as interactive signage and professional uses. Large interactive displays can serve in public places for many purposes. One of the applications is shopping mall directory – visitors can access information with minimum actions through the large display and the information can be well customized targeting to different visitors as the visitors can interactive with it. Moreover, in the perspective of retailer stores, the large interactive display is more than just attract visitors’ attention, it can help them collecting information of visitors’ usage of the directory, hence make marketing strategies based on these feedback. Large interactive displays can be served in government and corporate by providing new means of collaboration in meetings and in educational centers, the use of large touch displays provides more intuitive and interactive ways to serve the knowledge to learners.

There are a variety of touch technologies for large displays - optical imaging, resistive, projected capacitive, bending wave, infrared (IR) and surface acoustic wave (SAW). Among them, optical imaging is promising. The main advantage of optical imaging based touch interface is the well-controlled cost for scaling. The interactive area can often be increased without significant increase in the system setup. Besides the cost effectiveness, the technology does not have too much limitation of the environment.

Through 3 years of research and development of the optical imaging technology. The team improved the accuracy, efficiency, stability as well as portability of the touch interface. The system has been successfully deployed in the newly opened Singapore River Safari as an interactive education platform.
